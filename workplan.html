Work Packages
The research activities are organized into six work packages covering the full two-year contract period. Each package represents a distinct but interconnected strand of work — from core XR development to dissemination, education, cloud infrastructure, smart glasses and international collaboration with XR labs.
01
WP1 — Core Research
XR Development — Spatially Intelligent Digital Twins
Oct 2025 → Oct 2027 (full contract)
The primary research strand — designing, building, testing and iterating immersive VR/MR/AR applications for all 9 DIWA supersites. UX research principles including persona development, journey mapping and user needs analysis guide the design process. The Oulankajoki Supersite is the first completed prototype and proof of concept for the full platform.
  • →Oulankajoki Supersite VR — complete, demo-ready for EGU Vienna May 2026
  • →All 9 DIWA supersites — point cloud upload and georeferencing via Cesium ion
  • →DIWA Virtual Hub — circular MR lobby with 9 rounded tabletop supersite displays
  • →Multiplayer shared XR — two Quest 3 headsets in same scene via Photon PUN 2
  • →Passthrough Mixed Reality portal anchored to physical table marker
  • →AI integration — voice commands, chatbot, terrain recognition, field guide
  • →UX research — personas, journey maps, user needs studies with DIWA researchers
  • →3D UI design principles applied to environmental digital twin interfaces
Active — ongoing throughout contract

04
WP4 — Cloud & AI
CSC, Azure, AWS and AI Integration for XR
Autumn 2026 → 2027
Leveraging the available cloud and HPC infrastructure — CSC Finland national supercomputers, Microsoft Azure (licensed practitioner) and Amazon AWS (licensed practitioner) — for advanced XR capabilities including AI model training on DIWA LiDAR datasets, shared MR anchors and cloud AI inference for the intelligent field guide system.
  • →CSC Puhti GPU cluster — ML model training on all 9 supersite LiDAR datasets
  • →Azure Spatial Anchors — shared MR portal synchronization between headsets
  • →Claude API / GPT-4 — AI chatbot and voice navigation embedded in Unity VR
  • →AWS SageMaker — environmental AI models from real DIWA sensor data
  • →5G edge computing experiments for low-latency XR streaming
Planned — starting autumn 2026
05
WP5 — Next Platform
Smart AR Glasses Field Research Platform for DIWA
2027 — forward-looking research
Investigating and prototyping the transition of DIWA Digital Twins from VR headsets to smart AR glasses — enabling field researchers to access georeferenced data overlays while physically present at DIWA supersites. Aligned with the 2027 consumer AR glasses release wave from Meta, Google, Samsung and Apple.
  • →Rokid / Xreal Unity SDK experiments — near-term prototyping
  • →Android XR glasses — Google / Samsung platform evaluation
  • →Cesium ion geospatial data overlay on AR glasses display
  • →Remote field collaboration via AR glasses and shared spatial anchors
  • →Meta Artemis consumer glasses — 2027 research opportunity assessment
Planned — 2027 research phase
06
WP6 — International Collaboration
XR Lab Partnerships & External Research Networks
2026 → Oct 2027 (ongoing)
Actively establishing communication and collaboration channels with XR research laboratories at other universities and research institutions internationally. The goal is to position DIWA XR research within the broader European and global XR research community, share methodologies and explore joint projects and publications.
  • →Identify and contact XR labs at European universities working on similar themes
  • →Participate in EuroXR, ISMAR and UnitedXR networks as entry points
  • →Explore joint publications with external XR research groups
  • →Establish bilateral research exchanges where possible
  • →Connect DIWA with the broader spatial computing research ecosystem
  • →Industry partner connections via Slush and Immersive Tech Week events
Planned — starting 2026
The six work packages are designed to be mutually reinforcing — research outputs from WP1 feed directly into WP2 dissemination, WP3 educational content draws on WP1 methodology, and WP6 collaboration creates opportunities to expand WP1 scope and WP4 infrastructure partnerships.

Educational Content — Video Course Plan
Three short video courses are planned for production starting summer 2026. The courses are designed as pre-recorded content — each video is approximately 15 minutes long — making them suitable for self-paced online learning. They will be offered as University of Oulu PhD course credits for doctoral students and research staff, and made available as open educational resources for the wider XR and research community. The production approach keeps filming and editing manageable alongside the main research activities.
🎨
Course 01 — 3 × 15 min videos
Immersive UI/UX Design for Digital Twins
Pre-recorded  ·  PhD course eligible  ·  Open access
Goal: Teach the principles of designing user interfaces and experiences for XR digital twin environments — covering spatial interaction design, 3D UI patterns and the unique challenges of immersive environments.
→XR interaction design principles and patterns
→Persona development and user needs in XR
→Journey mapping for immersive environments
→3D UI patterns for digital twin interfaces
→Accessibility and presence in VR and MR
→Case study: DIWA Oulankajoki Digital Twin
🥽
Course 02 — 1 × 15 min video
XR Core Values for DIWA
Pre-recorded  ·  PhD course eligible  ·  DIWA internal + open
Goal: Introduce foundational principles of extended reality in the DIWA digital twin context — helping DIWA researchers understand spatial computing and its direct relevance to environmental science monitoring and communication.
→What is XR and why it matters for DIWA
→VR vs AR vs MR — which for which purpose
→Spatially Intelligent Digital Twins explained
→Cognitive XR and environmental education
→The DIWA Virtual Hub vision
→From field data to immersive experience

Course 03 — 1 × 15 min video
Tech Solopreneurship
Pre-recorded  ·  Open access  ·  Startup community
Goal: Enable researchers and students to turn XR skills and research expertise into real-world opportunities — bridging academic research and entrepreneurship in the spatial computing space.

All three courses will be filmed in a consistent visual style and hosted on a platform accessible to University of Oulu students and staff as well as external learners. They are timed to be produced during periods of lower conference activity — primarily summer and early autumn 2026 — so they do not compete with the main research and dissemination workload.

Future Career Expectations — University of Oulu & DIWA
Long-term Vision: Senior Researcher — DIWA XR Lab Director
Beyond the current two-year contract, the aspiration is to establish a permanent and secure senior research position within the DIWA Flagship Program and the IDT Lab at the University of Oulu. The long-term vision is to grow the XR research strand into a formally recognized, independently funded laboratory — the DIWA XR Lab — officially registered within the IDT Lab structure. This trajectory is built on the foundation of the current contract work, with each activity contributing to the track record, visibility and network required to secure long-term positions and funding.
2025–2027
Current Contract — Research Specialist / Postdoctoral Researcher
Establishing the XR research strand within DIWA. Building the technical foundation (Oulankajoki Digital Twin, all 9 supersites, DIWA Virtual Hub), disseminating at international conferences, developing educational content and building the external network. This period lays the groundwork for all future career development.
2027–2028
Promotion to Senior Researcher — DIWA XR Research Lead
Securing a senior researcher position within DIWA or IDT Lab — either through contract renewal and promotion, external grant funding, or a tenure-track position. At this stage the XR research strand is sufficiently established to justify a dedicated senior role. Publications, conference presence, industry connections and the educational courses all support this promotion case.
2028–2029
Establishing the DIWA XR Lab — Officially within IDT Lab
Formally establishing the DIWA XR Lab as a recognized research unit within the IDT Lab at the University of Oulu. This includes securing dedicated infrastructure (hardware, space, staff), an official name and registration, and a sustainable funding model combining DIWA programme funding, external grants and industry partnerships. The lab would serve as a hub for XR research in environmental science across Finland and the Nordic region.
2029–2030
Running DIWA XR Lab — Principal Investigator / Lab Director
Operating as Principal Investigator and director of the DIWA XR Lab — leading a small team of researchers, PhD students and collaborators. Securing competitive external grants (Academy of Finland, Horizon Europe, Business Finland). Expanding the lab's scope to all 9 DIWA supersites and potentially other Finnish and Nordic environmental monitoring sites. Establishing the lab as the leading center for environmental XR research in the Nordic region.
2030+
Secured Senior Position — Established Research Leader at University of Oulu
A permanent, tenured or long-term secured position at the University of Oulu — either as Associate Professor in XR/Spatial Computing or as a permanently funded Senior Research Fellow within IDT Lab and DIWA. The DIWA XR Lab is independently funded, internationally recognized, and serves as a model for how spatial computing can transform environmental science research communication and public engagement.
The career trajectory described above is aspirational and reflects long-term ambition rather than confirmed plans. Each stage depends on successful delivery of the current contract, publication output, external funding acquisition and the continued growth and relevance of XR research within the DIWA programme and the University of Oulu strategic priorities.
